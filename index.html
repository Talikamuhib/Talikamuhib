Kidney Stones: Vision Meets Medicine

Millions suffer from kidney stones without early detection. The pain is sudden. The diagnosis is delayed. I asked: What if computers could see what doctors sometimes miss? Using computer vision and deep learning on medical imaging, I built a system that identifies kidney stones with precision and speed. Every early detection is a patient spared from unnecessary suffering. This is why I code: to translate human pain into algorithmic solutions.

By the time Alzheimer's is diagnosed, the brain has already changed. The window for intervention closes. I decided to open it wider.

About GEM 2017

The Global Entrepreneurship Monitor (GEM) is the world's leading study of entrepreneurial dynamics. The 2017 wave captures individual-level data on entrepreneurial intentions, business ownership, business attitudes, and demographics across diverse economic contexts. This dataset represents approximately 190,000 adults aged 18-64 surveyed across 71 economies.

Key Insights & Analysis

Entrepreneurial Intentions

Analyze planned entrepreneurship across regions. Identify countries with highest intention-to-action conversion rates.

Business Ownership

Current business ownership rates by country, industry, and demographic. Early-stage vs established patterns.

Demographics

Age, gender, education, and employment status profiles of entrepreneurs globally. Identify underrepresented groups.

Motivation & Skills

Necessity-driven vs opportunity-driven entrepreneurship. Self-perceived business skills and knowledge assessment.

Regional Patterns

Entrepreneurship dynamics by development level. Emerging markets vs developed economies comparison.

Policy Implications

Support assessment, government policies, financing availability. Barriers to entrepreneurship identification.

Dataset Variables

Core Measures

  • Entrepreneurial Intentions: Plans to start business in next 3 years
  • TEA (Total Early-stage Entrepreneurial Activity): Nascent + new business ownership
  • Business Ownership: Active ownership of established businesses (3.5+ years)
  • Demographics: Age, gender, education level, employment status, income
  • Motivation: Opportunity vs necessity-driven entrepreneurship assessment
  • Skills: Perceived capabilities in business skills and knowledge
  • Fear of Failure: Fear of startup failure rate by country
  • Attitudes: Social desirability, business status, and opportunity recognition

Motor Claims CourtPack AI

AI-powered prototype for automating motor insurance court pack analysis. Extracts structured claim data from unstructured legal documents, provides explainable insights, and flags risks for faster, more accurate claims workflows.

The Challenge

Motor insurance claims involve large, unstructured court bundles with complex legal language. Manual document review is time-consuming, error-prone, and creates audit concerns. Insurance teams need to extract key claim fields, identify inconsistencies, and flag fraud risks‚Äîall while maintaining explainability for compliance.

Solution Overview

Document Intake

Simple Streamlit interface for uploading court pack PDFs. Supports batch processing for enterprise workflows.

Structured Extraction

Automatically extracts accident date, hire duration, vehicle registration, cost details with page-level evidence.

Confidence Scoring

Each extracted field includes confidence score for decision support‚Äînot replacement.

Anomaly Detection

Rule-based validation flags timeline inconsistencies, cost anomalies, and potential fraud indicators.

Risk Indicators

Generates early triage flags for high-risk claims requiring manual review and investigation.

Export Options

Results exportable as JSON and CSV for downstream processing and integration with claims systems.

Key Features

1

PDF Upload & Parsing

Drag-and-drop interface using Streamlit. PyMuPDF extracts text and metadata from complex multi-page PDFs with preservation of page-level information.

2

Field Extraction

Identifies and extracts: accident date, injury duration, vehicle registration, repair costs, hire duration, medical expenses. Rule-based parsing with Pydantic validation.

3

Evidence Linking

Each extracted field includes source page and relevant text snippet for audit trail and manual verification.

4

Consistency Checks

Validates timeline logic (start/end dates), cost ranges, medical coherence. Flags logical inconsistencies for investigation.

5

Risk Scoring

Calculates risk indicators based on cost thresholds, missing fields, inconsistencies, and historical patterns.

6

Triage & Export

Generates priority scores for manual review. Exports structured JSON/CSV for downstream workflow integration and compliance systems.

Why This Approach

MVP Philosophy

Lightweight, deployable in days. Focuses on practical automation rather than perfect AI‚Äîhuman expertise remains central.

Explainability

Every decision is traceable to source documents. Supports compliance audits and builds trust with claims teams.

Risk Reduction

Rule-based validation prevents systematic errors. Confidence scores prevent over-reliance on automation.

Scalability

Python + Streamlit deployment ready. Can process hundreds of court packs daily on standard infrastructure.

Compliance-Ready

Audit trail built in. Transparent decision logic satisfies regulatory requirements and legal standards.

User-Centric

Streamlit interface requires no ML expertise. Claims adjusters can use immediately after brief training.

Business Impact

Expected Outcomes

  • Speed: Reduce manual document review from 2-3 hours to 15-30 minutes per claim
  • Consistency: Eliminate human review variance; standardize field extraction across teams
  • Accuracy: Rule-based validation catches logical inconsistencies humans might miss
  • Cost: Enable junior adjusters to handle routine claims; free experts for complex cases
  • Fraud Prevention: Early risk signals enable targeted investigation of high-risk claims
  • Compliance: Full audit trail satisfies FCA and internal governance requirements

Technical Stack

Core Technologies

  • Frontend: Streamlit for interactive, no-code web interface
  • Backend: Python with pandas, PyMuPDF for PDF parsing and data processing
  • Data Validation: Pydantic for type safety and structured output
  • Deployment: Lightweight containerization; runs on standard business infrastructure
  • License: MIT‚Äîopen source and community-friendly
